# Spoken Bangalore Kannada Curriculum Map

## Learner Goal

Target level: Social Kannada.

By the end of the course, the learner should be able to handle normal daily life in Bangalore and hold simple casual conversations with neighbours, shopkeepers, apartment staff, auto/cab drivers, delivery agents, colleagues, and new friends.

The course focuses on speaking and listening only. Kannada script is never used. Kannada appears only in Latin transliteration, with Hindi Devanagari pronunciation support.

## Fluency Target

The learner should be able to:

- Build Kannada sentences from reusable patterns instead of memorizing isolated phrases.
- Ask and answer practical questions in real Bangalore situations.
- Understand common natural replies, including faster local speech and common contractions.
- Use mostly Kannada, while keeping natural English loan words such as auto, cab, phone, office, bill, parcel, and Colgate.
- Combine patterns to explain needs, problems, plans, preferences, locations, time, quantity, money, and simple stories.
- Move gradually from Hindi-supported learning toward Kannada-first thinking.

## Course Length Decision

Recommended structure: 72 lessons.

Why 72:

- 60 lessons is enough for survival and routine daily Kannada, but slightly tight for social confidence and listening variation.
- 80-100 micro-lessons would be granular, but too many tiny files may make revision scattered.
- 72 gives a clean 12-module structure with 6 lessons per module.

Each lesson is designed for about 20 minutes:

- 3 minutes: quick recall
- 8 minutes: new pattern and examples
- 6 minutes: production practice
- 3 minutes: roleplay or quiz

Daily revision target: 10 minutes using `tracking/daily-review.md`, `tracking/patterns.md`, and `tracking/vocab.md`.

## Lesson Format

Each lesson should contain:

1. Quick Recall
2. New Words
3. Main Pattern
4. Sentence Breakdown
5. Construction Logic
6. Reusable Pattern
7. Guided Practice
8. Production Practice
9. Real-Life Bangalore Roleplay
10. Quiz
11. Add These To Your Notes

Each answer file should contain:

1. Guided Practice Answers
2. Production Practice Sample Answers
3. Roleplay Sample
4. Quiz Answers
5. Natural Bangalore Versions
6. Common Mistakes

## Memory Budget Per Lesson

Hard maximum:

- 10 new words
- 2 sentence patterns
- 1 tiny grammar idea

Preferred:

- 5-8 new words
- 1 strong reusable pattern
- 1 real-life situation

## Module Plan

### Module 1: Survival Building Blocks

Lessons 001-006.

Goal: Speak simple object, need, yes/no, and question sentences.

Core abilities:

- Say this/that/here/there.
- Ask what something is.
- Say want and do not want.
- Ask simple yes/no questions.
- Use polite basic replies.

Main situations:

- Tea stall
- Apartment gate
- Shop counter
- Office reception

### Module 2: Location And Movement

Lessons 007-012.

Goal: Ask where things are and understand directions.

Core abilities:

- Ask where a place/person/object is.
- Say here, there, nearby, inside, outside.
- Ask someone to come, go, wait, and show.
- Understand simple direction replies.

Main situations:

- Security guard
- Office floor
- Apartment building
- Finding shops and services

### Module 3: Buying, Giving, Taking

Lessons 013-018.

Goal: Shop, request items, ask prices, and manage quantities.

Core abilities:

- Ask for items.
- Say give me, take this, keep it.
- Ask how much.
- Say one/two/some/more/less/enough.
- Handle billing and bags.

Main situations:

- Grocery store
- Pharmacy
- Vegetable shop
- Tea and snacks

### Module 4: Auto, Cab, And Travel

Lessons 019-024.

Goal: Handle common transport conversations.

Core abilities:

- Say where to go.
- Ask if someone will come.
- Discuss route, meter, traffic, left/right/straight.
- Ask to stop, wait, turn, or go slowly.
- Handle pickup/drop location issues.

Main situations:

- Auto drivers
- Cab drivers
- Metro and bus
- Travel inside Bangalore

### Module 5: People And Daily Work

Lessons 025-030.

Goal: Talk about people, work, home, and daily actions.

Core abilities:

- Say I/you/he/she/we/they in practical spoken forms.
- Talk about home, office, work, food, water, cleaning, coming, going.
- Ask what someone is doing.
- Give simple instructions politely.

Main situations:

- Housekeeping
- Maintenance staff
- Office colleagues
- Neighbours

### Module 6: Time, Plans, And Routine

Lessons 031-036.

Goal: Talk about time, schedules, and repeated actions.

Core abilities:

- Use today, tomorrow, yesterday, now, later, morning, evening.
- Ask when something will happen.
- Say I will come/go/do.
- Talk about routine: daily, sometimes, usually.

Main situations:

- Delivery timing
- Office plans
- Apartment repairs
- Meeting neighbours

### Module 7: Problems And Requests

Lessons 037-042.

Goal: Explain problems and ask for help naturally.

Core abilities:

- Say something is not working.
- Explain water, electricity, internet, door, lift, payment, delivery problems.
- Ask someone to check, fix, call, send, bring, or tell.
- Escalate politely.

Main situations:

- Apartment maintenance
- Delivery agents
- Customer support in person
- Office admin

### Module 8: Past Events

Lessons 043-048.

Goal: Explain what happened.

Core abilities:

- Say I went, came, bought, gave, called, saw, told.
- Ask what happened.
- Explain simple mistakes or delays.
- Combine past with time words.

Main situations:

- Missed delivery
- Late cab
- Payment issue
- Office and neighbour small talk

### Module 9: Preferences, Feelings, And Opinions

Lessons 049-054.

Goal: Move beyond transactions into social Kannada.

Core abilities:

- Say I like/do not like.
- Say good, bad, okay, difficult, easy, tasty, enough.
- Ask someone’s preference.
- Give simple opinions.

Main situations:

- Food and restaurants
- Friends and colleagues
- Local recommendations
- Shopping choices

### Module 10: Social Conversation

Lessons 055-060.

Goal: Hold short friendly conversations.

Core abilities:

- Ask about family, work, place, language, food, weekend, and plans.
- Answer common personal questions.
- Make small talk without sounding like a textbook.
- Use natural polite fillers and responses.

Main situations:

- Neighbours
- Colleagues
- Apartment staff
- Making friends

### Module 11: Listening And Natural Speech

Lessons 061-066.

Goal: Recognize natural Bangalore Kannada variants.

Core abilities:

- Understand common shortened forms.
- Recognize fast replies to common questions.
- Practice Kannada-English mixed situations while keeping Kannada as the base.
- Learn multiple natural ways to say the same thing.

Main situations:

- Fast shopkeeper replies
- Auto driver negotiations
- Security guard instructions
- Casual office speech

### Module 12: Combined Real-Life Scenarios

Lessons 067-072.

Goal: Integrate all previous patterns into realistic conversations.

Core abilities:

- Handle multi-turn conversations.
- Ask follow-up questions.
- Recover when you do not understand.
- Explain a full situation in simple Kannada.
- Speak with confidence even when imperfect.

Main situations:

- Full auto ride
- Full grocery visit
- Apartment repair issue
- Delivery confusion
- Restaurant order
- Social chat with neighbour or colleague

## Tracking Files

### `daily-review.md`

Very concise daily revision. It should contain only the highest-value patterns, must-memorize words, and mini-dialogues.

### `patterns.md`

All reusable patterns grouped by function: questions, need/want, location, movement, time, past, future, problems, opinions, and social conversation.

### `vocab.md`

Vocabulary grouped by practical function, not alphabetically.

Buckets:

- Must Memorize
- Familiarize
- Exposure Only

### `pronunciation.md`

Pronunciation rules for Hindi speakers, including Kannada sounds that do not map perfectly to Hindi.

### `survival-dialogues.md`

Short reusable dialogues for daily Bangalore situations.

## Maintenance Strategy

The course is maintained directly through the lesson, answer, and tracking files.

When revising lessons, update the matching answer files and any affected tracking files:

- `daily-review.md`
- `patterns.md`
- `vocab.md`
- `survival-dialogues.md`

Keep the repository focused on static learning material unless additional tooling is explicitly requested.
