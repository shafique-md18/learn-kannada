# Learn Spoken Bangalore Kannada

A practical 72-lesson course for learning spoken Bangalore Kannada.

This course is built for a learner who knows Hindi and English, lives in Bangalore, and wants speaking/listening ability for daily life. It does not teach Kannada script.

## Goal

The goal is social daily-life Kannada:

- Talk to auto/cab drivers
- Talk to shopkeepers and restaurant staff
- Talk to security guards, delivery agents, housekeeping, and maintenance staff
- Handle apartment and office situations
- Make small talk with neighbours and colleagues
- Start thinking in reusable Kannada sentence patterns

## Course Rules

- Kannada is written only in Latin transliteration.
- Kannada script is not used.
- Hindi Devanagari is used only for pronunciation support.
- Lessons focus on sentence construction, not phrase memorization.
- English loan words are kept where they are natural in Bangalore speech, such as auto, cab, bill, office, UPI, lift, and parcel.

## How To Study

Recommended routine:

1. Open one lesson from `lessons/`.
2. Do the quick recall aloud.
3. Study the new words and main pattern.
4. Complete the guided and production practice without checking answers.
5. Check the matching file in `answers/`.
6. Add weak words or patterns to your own notes.
7. Review `tracking/daily-review.md` for 10 minutes.

Each lesson is designed for about 20 minutes.

## Recommended Pace

Use the course flexibly:

- Fast day: 3-10 lessons
- Normal day: 1-2 lessons
- Tired day: only review tracking files

Do not rush past weak patterns. The goal is automatic spoken use, not finishing files.

## Tracking Files

- `daily-review.md`: concise high-value revision
- `patterns.md`: reusable sentence patterns grouped by function
- `vocab.md`: vocabulary grouped by usefulness and situation
- `pronunciation.md`: pronunciation help for Hindi speakers
- `survival-dialogues.md`: short practical Bangalore dialogues
- `curriculum-map.md`: full course map and module plan

## Using A Practice Assistant

After a block of lessons, use another assistant or speaking partner for practice.

Good prompt:

```text
Use lessons 001-012, tracking/patterns.md, and tracking/vocab.md.
Quiz me in spoken Bangalore Kannada.
Do not use Kannada script.
Correct me with:
Your Version / Correct Version / Natural Bangalore Version.
```

## Notes

The course prioritizes practical Bangalore Kannada over textbook Kannada. Some sentences intentionally use natural Kannada-English mixed speech when that is what people commonly use in daily Bangalore conversations.
